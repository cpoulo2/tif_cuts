What we know (the big picture)

  • Voters prefer investment over cuts: When asked to choose between cutting education or taxing corporations, over 80% of Chicagoans choose taxing ultra-wealthy corporations.
  • Even stronger support in communities of color: More than 90% of Black and Latine voters prefer taxing corporations over cutting education.
  • Voters reward investment: Polling shows voter favorability rises for Alders who support equitable funding increases.
  • Protecting Chicago invests–it doesn't cut: Mayor Brandon Johnson's budget protects against Trump cuts and expands youth jobs, healthcare, and violence prevention by both making ultra-wealthy corporations pay a community safety surcharge and delivering a historic billion-dollar TIF surplus to schools, parks, and libraries.
  • Chicago's largest corporations received over $100 million in property tax cuts this year alone and will receive billions in Trump tax cuts.
  • A 'no' vote means cuts: Voting "no" to TIF surplus and the Community Safety Surcharge is a vote for cuts, period. A no vote takes money from youth jobs and programming, books for libraries, and gender-based violence reduction programs.
  • A 'yes' vote means investing in our youth and city: Voting for TIF surplus and the Community Safety Surcharge means investing in our schools and city by utilizing unused TIF funds and making ultra-rich corporations pay their fair share.
